Black Bean Soup

Ready in 40 minutes
Serves 4 people

Tips

The longer you cook the soup base “low and slow” the better this soup will turn out. Blend half or all the soup depending on your preference for texture. This goes great with some hot quesadillas, some tortilla chips, or just on its own.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans of black beans
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 bell peppers
  • 3 stalks celery
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ a bunch of cilantro
  • 2 serrano chillies
  • 4 cups vegetable stock (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano

Preparation

  1. Cook your soup base. Saute the finely diced onion and celery in a few tablespoons of olive oil.
  2. Once the onion is translucent, add the bay leaf and finely diced bell pepper.
  3. Cook the vegetables through until they’re softened. Add dried oregano, minced garlic, finely chopped cilantro stems, and finely minced serrano chillies. Cook it out a few minutes.
  4. Add the beans and stock. Add in the canned beans, without draining. Add vegetable stock or water and let it come to a boil.
  5. Let the soup boil for at least 10-15 minutes. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Blend with an immersion blender for a smoother texture.
  7. Serve hot. Garnish with cilantro and some avocado chunks. Some lime wedges on the side and hot sauce is great too.
