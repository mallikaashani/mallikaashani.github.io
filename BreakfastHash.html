Recipe

Breakfast Hash

Ready in 40 minutes
Serves 6 people

Tips

This is pretty customizable, so use whatever beans and vegetables you have. Some good ones would include corn, zucchini, sweet potato, or even some greens like kale. You can add chorizo/soyrizo in the beginning of the recipe as well.

Ingredients

  • 4 large potatoes
  • 1 medium onion
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 large bell pepper
  • 2 cans of black beans
  • 1 large jalapeno
  • 8 oz mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese for topping
  • Breadcrumbs for topping (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Eggs for serving
  • Cilantro for garnish (opt)
  • Avocado for serving (opt)

Preparation

  1. Prepare the potatoes. This is the important part. Peel and chop the potato into bite size chunks.
  2. Add a bit of water to a large bowl with the potatoes, cover, and microwave for 4 minutes. The potato pieces should be 90% cooked. Fork tender or very close.
  3. Add the potatoes to a skillet on medium heat with 2 tablespoons oil.
  4. Let the potatoes get brown and crispy. Don't stir too much initially. Once they are crisped up to your liking, remove them from the pan and set aside.
  5. Prepare the vegetables. Add oil to your skillet as needed and sauted the sliced onions and peppers.
  6. As the onions become translucent, add in minced garlic, jalapeno, and sliced mushrooms.
  7. Saute on medium high heat until the edges of all the vegetables are browned. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Assemble. Add the crisped potatoes back in, mix through to distribute evenly.
  9. Drain and rinse the beans. Distribute them evenly on the skillet.
  10. Sprinkle on the cheese and optional breadcrumbs evenly.
  11. Cover the skillet until the cheese has melted. If you're adding breadcrumbs, use the broiler to get a nice crust on the whole dish.
  12. Add on your garnishes- this can be cilantro, diced avocado, any salsas or hot sauces you like, or thinly sliced radishes.
  13. Serve with eggs any style!
