Oatmeal is healthy, filling, and cheap- but it can also get boring. Here are four different ways I like to make rolled oats- which I generally keep on hand. These recipes are easy and quick to make on a Sunday evening for the week.

Banana Oat Pancakes

Ready in 15 minutes
Serves 4 people

Tips

My favorite topping combinations are: (1) thinly sliced ripe nectarines and almonds with drizzle of maple syrup (2) sliced bananas with chocolate chips and chopped peanuts. For a treat, top with nutella and strawberries.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup oat flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tsps cinnamon
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds (optional)
  • ¼ cup mix ins (optional)
  • Butter for greasing

Preparation

  1. Prepare the batter. Mash the bananas, crack in eggs, mix till combined. Add chia seeds.
  2. Stir in vanilla, cinnamon, baking powder.
  3. Mix in oat flour and add ins of choice. Add a splash of milk of choice if batter is too thick.
  4. Cook your pancakes. Heat the pan to medium heat. Add butter to coat pan.
  5. Add roughly 4 tablespoons of batter per pancake and spread to desired thickness. Do not push down on the pancake.
  6. Cook 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through.
  7. Serve with choice of toppings. Ex. drizzle of maple syrup, honey, yogurt, fruit, nuts, chocolate, etc.
Recipe

Maple Granola

Ready in 45 minutes
Cook time 35 minutes
Serves 8 people

Tips

My favorite ways to eat are: (1) on top of some plain yogurt with berries (2) with almond milk, sliced bananas, and drizzle of honey. Granola stays good for at least a month.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups old fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 ½ cups of chopped nuts
  • ½ cup pumpkin seeds
  • ⅓ cup maple syrup (add 2-3 tbsp for extra sweetness)
  • ¼ cup coconut oil
  • 2 tsps cinnamon
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ⅓ cup dried fruit

Preparation

  1. Prepare the dry ingredients. Add oats, chopped nuts, and seeds to a bowl. Toss to distribute ingredients. (For nuts, I use walnuts and almonds.)
  2. Prepare the wet ingredients. Stir together heated coconut oil, vanilla, cinnamon, salt, and maple syrup.
  3. Toss to evenly coat dry ingredients with wet ingredients.
  4. Roast your granola. Preheat oven to 350° F. Spread granola evenly on a baking sheet. The layer of oats should be ½ inch thick or less.
  5. After 30 minutes, turn off your oven and allow granola to start cooling inside oven. This helps with crispiness.
  6. When cooled, toss with dried fruit. Enjoy with yogurt, milk, fruit, etc.

Breakfast Bars

Ready in 30 minutes
Cook time 20 minutes
Serves 6 people

Tips

Store these in the fridge. Eat them hot or cold, with a cup of tea or coffee.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups old fashioned rolled oats
  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 2 tbsp nut butter
  • 2 tsps cinnamon
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds (optional)
  • ⅓ cup add ins (nuts, chocolate chips, etc.) (optional)

Preparation

  1. Prepare the mixture. Mash the bananas. Add nut butter and whisk together.
  2. Add in vanilla, cinnamon, salt, and chia seeds.
  3. Fold in oats and add ins, until mixture is evenly combined. Add in a splash of milk of choice if mixture is too stiff.
  4. Bake your bars. Preheat oven to 350° F. Spread mixture evenly on a baking sheet, about ½ inch thick.
  5. Bake in the oven till golden brown around the edges.
  6. Allow to cool slightly. Cut into squares and enjoy.
  7. Serve with choice of toppings. Ex. drizzle of maple syrup, honey, yogurt, fruit, nuts, chocolate, etc.

Overnight Oats 3 Ways

Ready in 6-12 hours
Prep time 10 minutes
Serves 2 people

Tips

Heat up your oats in the morning, add a splash of milk to thin as needed.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups old fashioned rolled oats
  • 2 cups milk of choice
  • 1 tbsp liquid sweetener (maple syrup, agave, honey)
  • 1 tbsp nut butter
  • 2 tsps cinnamon
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds (optional)
  • ⅓ cup add ins (nuts, chocolate chips, etc.) (optional)
  • ⅓ cup fruit (apple, mixed berries, etc.)

Preparation

  1. Combine ingredients. Whisk together nut butter, liquid sweetener, milk of choice, vanilla, and salt.
  2. Mix in oats and chia seeds. Let it sit for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  3. Customize. For apple pie oats, mix in almond butter, chopped apple, chopped pecans, and cinnamon.
  4. For chocolate-banana oats, mix in peanut butter, cocoa powder, mini chocolate chips, chopped peanuts, and sliced banana.
  5. For mixed berry oats, mix in frozen mixed berries, cardamom, almond butter, and sliced almonds.
