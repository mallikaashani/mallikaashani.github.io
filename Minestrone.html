Minestrone Soup

Ready in 35 minutes
Serves 4 people

Tips

This a customizable soup. I generally add zucchini, some kind of greens, and some beans (chickpeas, white beans, kidney beans). The best tomatoes are, however, essential as they flavor the broth. Taste and adjust as you go.

Ingredients

  • 1 can of San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1 can of beans
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 carrots
  • 3 stalks of celery
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • Â¼ cup of pasta
  • 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning
  • 3 cups of chopped kale or cabbage
  • 1 zucchini
  • 1 tsp red chili flakes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (opt.)

Preparation

  1. Make a sofrito. Heat olive oil to medium high heat. Add in finely diced onion, carrot, and celery.
  2. Once onions are translucent, add minced garlic, salt, pepper, Italian seasoning, and chili flakes. Cook out a few minutes.
  3. Add beans and veggies. Add canned San Marzano tomatoes and 4 cups of water or broth. Add water as needed. Let it come to a boil.
  4. Add diced zucchini, drained beans, and any other vegetables of your choice. Let them heat through and bring soup back to boil. Taste for salt.
  5. Add in your kale or cabbage and the pasta. The pasta will absorb the water, so add as necessary.
  6. Serve hot with some crusty bread. Add fresh basil and a drizzle of olive oil on top.
