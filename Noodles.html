Vegetable Soba Noodles

Ready in 35 minutes
Serves 4 people

Tips

The vegetables in this dish can easily be switched out for whatever you like: bell pepper, green beans, spinach, zucchini, kale, or snap peas. Frozen vegetables work too. This sauce is customizable as well- you can add acidic, spicy, and aromatic flavorings of your choice.

Ingredients

  • Soba noodles
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 cups broccoli
  • 3 green onions, sliced (optional)
  • Half packet of tofu
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Sambal or Sriracha
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon honey or agave (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sesame seeds for topping (optional)
  • Cilantro for garnish (optional)
  • 2 eggs (optional)

Preparation

  1. Cook the noodles and vegetables. Add the soba noodles to boiling salted water. Once the noodles have softened, add the sliced carrots and broccoli florets.
  2. Drain and set aside once noodles are mostly cooked through.
  3. Make your sauce. Grate ginger and garlic into a small bowl. Add soy sauce, sesame oil, honey, and sambal.
  4. Put it all together. In a super hot pan, fry up your cubed tofu in a bit of oil. When browned to your liking, add the white parts of green onion.
  5. Once the onion has turned softened, add the noodles and vegetables. Stir fry a minute or two.
  6. Add the sauce and stir to coat all the noodles. Add pepper and salt. Heat through for a few minutes.
  7. Serve hot with a garnishing of sesame seeds, cilantro, green onion, and a drizzle of hot sauce. Add fried eggs if you like.
