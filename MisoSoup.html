Miso soup is quick, easy and great when it's cold. You can have it when you want a light dinner or also easily make it more substantial.

Miso Soup

Ready in 15 minutes
Serves 2 people

Tips

You can add cooked soba noodles or some rice into this soup to make it a complete meal. You can also customize the vegetables depending on what you have. Also, the flavor of your miso really depends on the brand you get, etc. Taste and adjust once you've incorporated your miso.

Ingredients

  • 1 piece kombu (4-5 inch square)
  • 1 cup diced tofu
  • 1 thumb size piece of ginger
  • 3-4 baby bok choy
  • 2-3 tbsp yellow miso paste
  • 6-7 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 2-3 green onions

Preparation

  1. Create your broth. Add 6 cups of cold water to a pot. Add in the kombu and shiitake mushrooms.
  2. Let it come to a boil and cook for 3-5 minutes.
  3. Once the mushrooms have softened, take them out and slice them up. Leave the tough stems out.
  4. Remove the kombu. You can cut it into smaller pieces and add it back in if you would like.
  5. Add the vegetables. Add in chopped baby bok choy, thin strips of the ginger, and sliced mushrooms.
  6. Let the soup come to a boil and cook the bok choy to your liking. Add tofu cubes.
  7. Add the miso. Take the soup off the heat. This is important so you can get the benefits of miso.
  8. Use 2 spoons to dissolve the miso paste into the soup. Stir to incorporate. Add the sliced green onions.
  9. Serve hot. If you would like some soba noodles, add the cooked noodles now.
