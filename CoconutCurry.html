Indian Style Coconut Korma

Ready in 60 minutes
Serves 4 people

Tips

The basic sauce is very customizable. You can add kasoori methi or garam masala, change the ratio of spices, and add a different fat instead of coconut milk (ex. heavy cream). You can also add whatever vegetables you want. I use peas, green beans, and broccoli. Carrots, potatoes, and cauliflower would go well too.

Ingredients

  • 1 cans of coconut milk
  • 2 large onion
  • 3-4 medium tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup of cashews
  • 5-6 cloves garlic
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1-2 cinnamon sticks
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tablespoon turmeric
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 1/2 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 tablespoon ground cumin (optional)
  • 3-4 cups vegetables (depending on how saucy you want it)

Preparation

  1. Make the sauce. Roughly dice onions and tomatoes. Slice garlic and ginger.
  2. Saute onion, ginger, and garlic on medium high heat in 1-2 tablespoons of oil.
  3. Once the onion is translucent, add the tomatoes and cashews. Cook everything till tomatoes are soft.
  4. Blend the mixture into a smooth puree, adding water as necessary.
  5. Heat 1-2 tablespoons of oil, add in cinnamon sticks, cardamom pods, bay leaf, cloves, and cumin seeds.
  6. Once the whole spices have bloomed and the cumin seeds are sputtering, stir in the puree. Cook the puree while stirring frequently till it has thickened slightly.
  7. Season the sauce with salt, pepper (optional), and the ground spices (turmeric, chili powder, ground coriander, cumin powder).
  8. Continue cooking the sauce until the spices have cooked and there is no raw smell/flavor.
  9. Assemble. In a separate pot, boil your vegetables until 90% cooked.
  10. Add the boiled vegetables to the sauce along with coconut milk. Adjust consistency with water (and seasoning) as needed.
  11. Serve hot with naan, parathas, or rice.
