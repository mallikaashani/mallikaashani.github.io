Cauliflower Soup

Ready in 90 minutes
Serves 4 people

Tips

Adding the miso off the heat (once the soup is done boiling) helps retain its health benefits. Some crispy breadcrumbs or a gremolata would be great for serving!

Ingredients

  • 1 large cauliflower
  • 2 tablespoons of white miso
  • 3 heads of garlic
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper
  • Salt to taste

Preparation

  1. Roast. Preheat oven to 375.
  2. Cut the cauliflower into bite size florets. It's fine to include some of the tender stalk, cut it slightly smaller so it cooks through.
  3. Wash and pat dry the cauliflower. Toss with 2-3 tablespoons of olive oil and sprinkle with salt. This is the only fat going into this recipe, so you can be generous for a richer flavor.
  4. Cut the tops off the garlic heads, drizzle with some olive oil and sprinkle with salt. Wrap in foil.
  5. Roast cauliflower and foil wrapped garlic in the oven for 30 minutes.
  6. Remove garlic after 30 minutes. (Your garlic could require about 10 more minutes to roast if the cloves are larger.)
  7. Toss the cauliflower after 30 minutes, set the oven temperature to 400 and roast for 20-30 more minutes until cooked through. Some browning adds great flavor.
  8. Blend. Blend the cauliflower and softened garlic cloves until smooth with 2 cups of water to start. You can add water to get your desired thickness for the soup.
  9. Add this mixture into a pot to heat through, along with white pepper.
  10. Blend white miso with some water until smooth, and add to the soup once it has been heated. Mix through and add salt to taste.
  11. Serve hot!
