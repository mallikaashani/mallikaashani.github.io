Mexican Quinoa Skillet

Ready in 30 minutes
Serves 2 people

Tips

You can use any blend of vegetables or beans in here that you have. For example, you can use bell peppers instead of poblano pepper or zucchini instead of (or in addition to) the carrot. You can even add crumbled tempeh, tofu, or your meat of choice. This dish is great for lunch the next day as well- just sprinkle with a little water when reheating.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 poblano pepper
  • 1 carrot
  • 4 oz mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup corn
  • 1 can kidney or black beans
  • 1 tablespoon of taco seasoning
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheese
  • Cilantro for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving
  • 1 avocado for serving

Preparation

  1. Make the quinoa. Simmer the quinoa with 2 cups of water until cooked through.
  2. Cook the vegetables. In a skillet, saute the onion in 1-2 tablespoons of oil on medium high heat.
  3. Once the onion is translucent, add the minced garlic and the diced poblano pepper.
  4. When the onions are getting brown on the edges, add in the diced mushrooms, shredded (or finely diced) carrot, and half the taco seasoning. Cook until the mushrooms have released their water.
  5. Remove the mixture into a plate. Add a little more oil if needed. Add in the corn (I use frozen) and a can of drained and rinsed beans.
  6. Once the corn has started to soften, add in the remaining half of the taco seasoning. Saute until the corn has browned slightly and taken on a roasted flavor.
  7. Add the vegetable mixture from before back into the skillet. Mix to combine.
  8. Add in the cooked quinoa. Mix through and adjust for seasoning. You might need to add salt/pepper depending on your brand of taco seasoning.
  9. Prepare for serving. Top the whole skillet with a sprinkling of cheese. Turn off the heat and cover with a lid for the cheese to melt.
  10. Once cheese has melted, sprinkle with some cilantro. Serve with lime, sliced avocado, and hot sauce.
