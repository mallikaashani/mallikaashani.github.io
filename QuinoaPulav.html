I loved eating a warm bowl of pulav in the cold weather as a kid, here's a recipe that uses quinoa instead of rice. It's a pretty easy recipe that's perfect for weekday lunches. It pairs great with raita, kadhi, or a little pickle.

Recipe
Recipe

Quinoa Pulav

Ready in 30 minutes
Serves 4 people

Tips

I usually just a frozen mixed vegetable blend for this recipe. It includes carrots, corn, green beans, and peas. To cook quinoa, add 2 times the amount of water as quinoa.

Ingredients

  • 2 pods cardamom
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 5 black peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp asafoetida (opt.)
  • ½ tablespoon turmeric
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 medium onion
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups of chopped vegetables

Preparation

  1. Create an aromatic base. In a cold pan, add a cinnamon stick, cloves, peppercorns, cardamom, and a bay leaf to 3 tablespoons of oil or ghee. Put it in medium heat.
  2. Once up to temperature, add cumin seeds and asafoetida. When the cumin seeds start sputtering, add finely diced onion.
  3. When the onion is translucent, add grated ginger and minced (or grated) garlic. Let it cook out a few minutes.
  4. Add your vegetables. Add your chopped vegetables and salt to taste.
  5. As the vegetables soften, add turmeric and chili powder. Let it saute for a few minutes.
  6. Cook your quinoa. Add washed quinoa and saute a few minutes with vegetables and spices.
  7. Add 2 cups of water. Stir and cover. Cook for 20 minutes. Add water and cook longer if needed.
  8. Serve hot with raita or pickle.
